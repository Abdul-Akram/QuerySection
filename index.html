Frequently Asked Questions

Why is the moon sometimes out during the day?

Answer

When the moon is out during the day, it is because the moon is in the phase of the moon called the waxing crescent. The waxing crescent phase of the moon is when the moon is between the sun and the earth.

How do airplanes stay up?

Answer

Airplanes stay up because of the Bernoulli Principle. The Bernoulli Principle states that as the speed of a fluid increases, the pressure of the fluid decreases. The wings of an airplane are curved and as the air moves over the top of the wing, the air moves faster than the air moving under the wing. This causes the pressure on the top of the wing to decrease and the pressure on the bottom of the wing to increase. The difference in pressure causes the airplane to lift off the ground.
